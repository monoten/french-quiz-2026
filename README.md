# French Vocabulary Kit — Unités 1–6
Single-file practice tools for Dutch first-year high school students preparing for French vocabulary test (Unités 1–6). Works in any browser, on phone or laptop, no install required. 235 words across 16 themed topics.

## The three tools

- **[Quiz](https://monoten.github.io/french-quiz-2026/Quiz.html)** — Interactive quiz with themed filtering, typing or multiple-choice answers, leech-weighted question selection (recently-missed words come back more often), and .CSV file score export. Use this for daily active-recall practice & assessment.
- **[Flashcards](https://monoten.github.io/french-quiz-2026/flashcards.html)** — Printable fold-style flashcards (A4), organised into a Core deck (NL↔FR, bidirectional) and a Recognition deck (FR→NL only). Designed for use with a 5-box Leitner system (details on the page).
- **[Study booklet](https://monoten.github.io/french-quiz-2026/study-booklet.html)** — A topical study guide with 16 themed mini-'lessons'. Each theme has a word table, a French mini-story with Dutch translation, practice prompts, and notes on false friends and grammar quirks.

## Suggested use

Read a theme in the booklet, then run a quiz filtered to that theme, then do a Leitner pass on the flashcards. The three tools share the same 235 words and the same theme structure, so they reinforce each other.

**WARNING:** The single highest-yield activity is active recall: covering the Dutch side and trying to produce the French word (and vice versa for Core words) from memory; looking at the words on a page or screen & simply reading it in your mind is comforting & less painful, but does almost nothing. Active recall is critical and why the quiz is important - also important is to speak the words out loud as you read them, including in the quiz. Do that, test yourself, get it wrong, check the right answer and practice it until you get it right next time: that's how vocabulary sticks.

## Notes

- All scores, history, and settings are stored locally in your browser (`localStorage`) — per device, per browser. Nothing is uploaded.
- For flashcards and booklet, click the orange "Print / Save as PDF" button at the top of the page.
- Mobile-friendly (iPhone, Android) and desktop both work.
